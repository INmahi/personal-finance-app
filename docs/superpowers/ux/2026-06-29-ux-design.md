# XpenseTracker — UX Design

> Produced with the `ux-researcher-designer` skill.
> **Confidence: Low (exploratory).** This persona is assumption-based — the app
> serves the owner + a few invited people, not a researched population of 20+.
> Validate by watching real use and revising. Living document.

## 1. Primary persona — "Rafiq, the Aware Tracker"

**Archetype:** Casual-but-consistent personal tracker (not a power user).

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Context | Personal finances in Bangladesh; currency BDT (৳) |
| Devices | Phone first (Android, often offline/spotty data); occasional desktop/web |
| Tech proficiency | Medium |
| Frequency | Logs in bursts — a few entries a day, checks totals every few days |
| Money flows | In: salary, family support. Out: daily cash + bKash + bank spending |

**Goals**
- Know *"how much have I spent this month, and how much do I have left?"* at a glance.
- Capture an expense in seconds, even with no signal.
- Find a past transaction quickly; occasionally export expenses for records.

**Frustrations (assumed)**
- Generic apps are cluttered, ad-filled, or force categories/budgets he doesn't want.
- Re-login on every open; no offline capture.
- Totals buried behind taps.

**Design implications**
- → Lead with **two numbers**: spent-this-month and remaining money.
- → Make **add** and **summary** reachable in one tap; persistent session.
- → Category optional; never block a fast entry.
- → Light, calm, uncluttered; BDT formatting everywhere.

## 2. Journey map — "Check where I stand this month"

**Persona:** Rafiq · **Goal:** understand month-to-date spend + remaining ·
**Trigger:** opens app mid-month · **Success:** sees the two numbers and
(optionally) exports expenses · **Timeframe:** seconds.

| Stage | Actions | Emotion (1–5) | Pain point | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Open | Launches app, already logged in | 4 | Re-login would kill it | Persistent session |
| Orient | Sees home / opens **Summary** | 3 | "Where's the total?" | One-tap Summary entry |
| Reveal | Summary **slides in** with spent + remaining | 5 | Numbers feel hidden if buried | Big headline numbers, instant read |
| Drill | Toggles **expenses-only**, scans categories | 4 | Mixed in/out is confusing | Expenses-only scope toggle |
| Act | **Downloads CSV** of expenses | 4 | No way to get data out | One-tap CSV export |
| Close | Swipes/Esc to dismiss | 4 | Janky close | Smooth slide-out, scrim, reduced-motion fallback |

**Prioritized opportunities (Frequency × Severity × Solvability)**
1. Headline spent + remaining, instantly legible — **high**.
2. Expenses-only toggle within Summary — **high**.
3. One-tap CSV export of filtered expenses — **medium**.
4. Polished slide-over with reduced-motion fallback — **medium**.

## 3. The Summary slide-over — interaction spec

- **Entry:** a "Summary" tab/button. Panel enters from the right (RTL-safe:
  inline-end), ~280–320ms ease-out; dimmed scrim behind.
- **Headline (always visible):**
  - **Spent this month** = Σ expenses (`direction='out'`) with `occurred_on` in
    the current calendar month.
  - **Remaining money** = current balance = Σ in − Σ out (all-time).
    *(Interpretation choice — if you meant "this month's income − this month's
    spend," say so and we flip it. Recorded as an open question.)*
- **Scope toggle:** This month · Custom range.
- **Options:** **Expenses-only** view (hides income, shows by-category +
  by-payment-method breakdown of spend) · **Download CSV** (exports the currently
  filtered expense rows: date, category, payment method, amount, note).
- **Exit:** scrim tap · Esc · swipe; slide-out mirrors entry.
- **Accessibility:** focus trapped while open, returns focus on close; honors
  `prefers-reduced-motion` (fade/instant instead of slide); numbers have text
  labels, not color alone.

## 4. Light-theme direction (hand-off to `ui-design-system`)

Calm, high-contrast, finance-trustworthy light theme. To be turned into concrete
design tokens with the `ui-design-system` skill at Phase 2 plan time.

- **Surfaces:** near-white app bg, white cards, soft shadows over heavy borders.
- **Semantics:** income/in = green; expense/out = neutral-dark (not alarming
  red); destructive actions only = red. Never encode meaning by color alone.
- **Currency:** always `৳` prefix, `numeric(12,2)` formatting, thousands grouped.
- **Motion:** purposeful and short; respect reduced-motion.
- **Density:** generous tap targets (phone-first), legible type scale.

## 5. Validation checklist (when real users exist)
- [ ] Watch 3–5 invited users open the app and find spent + remaining unaided.
- [ ] Time "log an expense" — target < 10s.
- [ ] Confirm the "remaining money" definition matches their mental model.
- [ ] Test CSV export opens cleanly in their tool of choice.
